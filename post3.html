The Proposal Template That Gets Me 80% Response Rates

A great proposal doesn’t just list your skills — it makes the client feel like you already understand their problem. Here’s the structure I use that consistently gets replies.

Section 1 – Personalized Opening

  • Mention their business by name.
  • Reference something specific from their request or website.

Section 2 – Problem & Solution

  • Restate their problem in your own words.
  • Outline your solution in 3–4 bullet points.

Section 3 – Why Me

  • 2–3 sentences on your relevant experience.
  • Link to similar projects in your portfolio.

Section 4 – Timeline & Investment

  • Clear delivery phases (e.g., Design: 1 week, Development: 2 weeks).
  • Price with your three‑tier method.

Section 5 – Call to Action

  • Invite them to a quick call.
  • End with a confident but friendly close: “If this sounds good, I can get started as early as Monday.”

Keep it under 2 pages. Clients skim — make every word count.
